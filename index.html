The 20-Week Mass + Speed Blueprint

Build like a bodybuilder, move like a golfer.

Goal: add ~10 lb of lean muscle and convert it into clubhead speed. We front-load bodybuilding-style hypertrophy to put real size on your frame, then teach that new muscle to produce force — fast — and channel it through the ground and into rotation. The pros who've added 20+ mph did it by roughly doubling their force output, then training the body to apply it quickly. Pair this with the Lean Bulk macros — a clean, quality surplus, never a "dirty bulk."

🏗️

Build the Mass

High-volume hypertrophy on the big lifts puts 10 lb on your frame — bigger glutes, lats, quads and forearms are your force base. More muscle = a higher speed ceiling.

⚡

Make it Fast

Heavy strength then explosive Olympic-style and ballistic work teaches the new muscle to contract fast. Speed comes from rate of force development, not size alone.

🌀

Ground Force & Rotate

Push hard into the ground and fire the rotational chain. Ground-force footwork, med-ball throws, anti-rotation core and overspeed swings transfer raw power into the club.

📈 How to actually gain the 10 lb (and keep your swing)

  • Clean surplus, not a dirty bulk: use the Lean Bulk setting (+10%) and gain ~0.5–0.75 lb/week. Chasing 5,000 junk calories adds fat, wrecks your gut, and kills mobility — the leaner, faster body wins.
  • Hit protein every day: ~1g per lb of bodyweight, spread across your meals. Muscle is built in the kitchen.
  • Quality food & gut health: whole foods over processed; if you bloat or feel sluggish, watch common triggers (gluten, dairy, excess sugar). A good gut is how you actually absorb the fuel.
  • Progressive overload: add a little weight or a rep each week on the main lifts. Log every session.
  • Train the ground & the turn: push hard into the ground and rotate fast — that's where strength becomes clubhead speed. Never skip the 2–3 weekly speed/overspeed sessions.
  • Measure it: track clubhead speed on a radar and chase a new top-end number — what gets measured gets faster.
  • Protect mobility & sleep 7–9 hrs: keep thoracic-rotation and hip drills; recovery is where mass and speed are actually made.
